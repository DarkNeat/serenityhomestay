Waterfalls

____________________________________________

Jodu Ella

22 Km Away Gomara village Waterfalls

Also known as Bambarella Knuckles Falls, comprises twin waterfalls formed by the Moragaha River. Each fall is approximately 45 meters high, and is a popular destination for hikers exploring the Knuckles Range.

Gona Wetuna Ella (Milla Ella)

150 Km Away Badulla District Waterfalls

Also known as Milla Ella, this waterfall is hidden deep within the forest and is less frequented by tourists, offering a tranquil experience for visitors.

Huluganga Waterfall

25 Km Away Huluganga Town Waterfalls

Huluganga Falls originates from the Knuckles Mountain Range and is known for its scenic beauty. The waterfall gained popularity as a filming location for the teledrama “Ella Langa Walauwa.

Hunas Falls

20 Km Away Elkaduwa Waterfalls

Hunas Falls is a 48-meter-high man-made waterfall located in the landscaped garden of the Hunas Falls Hotel. It is fed by a stream from the nearby Hunnasgiriya mountain peak and is a popular tourist attraction.

Kaluwala Ella

180 Km Away Sabaragamuwa Province Waterfalls

Also known as Dumbara Kaluwala Ella, this small waterfall lies hidden inside the forests between Mathugama and Idangoda. It offers a serene environment for visitors seeking solitude.

Lebanon Waterfall

30 Km Away Kandy District waterfalls

The Lebanon Estate in Kandy District hides two large and beautiful waterfalls. These waterfalls are relatively unknown and have no official names, making them hidden gems for adventurous travelers.

Morella Waterfall

120 Km Away Samanala Range waterfalls

Moray Falls, located on the Moray Estate, is fed by a stream originating from the Samanala Mountain Range. The waterfall cascades in three segments before ending at the Muskeliya Reservoir.

Bambarella Saree Ella

22 Km Away Bambarella town waterfalls

Bambarella Saree Ella is named for its resemblance to a saree, the traditional dress of Sri Lankan women. The waterfall consists of several cascades and is accessible by any vehicle.​

Thaliya Wetunu Ella

27 Km Away Knuckles Mountain Range waterfalls

Thaliya Wetuna Ella, also known as Alakola Ella, is a 50-meter-high waterfall formed in a tributary of the Hulu Ganga. The area was once under a planter named Allen Collen during the British era, and the estate was later named Alakola Watte.

Historical Sites

____________________________________________

Gadaladeniya Vihara

12 Km away Pilimatalawa village historical site

Located just outside Kandy, Gadaladeniya Vihara is a 14th-century Buddhist temple known for its unique South Indian-inspired architecture and ancient stone carvings. It provides insight into the region's religious and artistic history and is a tranquil spot for visitors interested in heritage sites.

Kandy War Cemetery

17 Km away Kandy city historical site

This well-preserved cemetery is dedicated to soldiers from the British Empire who died during World War I and II. It offers a poignant glimpse into Sri Lanka's colonial past and is a peaceful place for reflection, just a short drive from central Kandy.

Lankatilaka Temple

22 Km away Udunuwara village historical site

This 14th-century Buddhist temple is renowned for its striking location atop a rock outcrop and its blend of Sinhalese and Dravidian architectural styles. The temple features impressive frescoes, sculptures, and panoramic views of the surrounding countryside, offering a serene and culturally rich experience.

Campbell’s Lane Forest Reserve

25 Km away Elkaduwa historical site

This forest reserve is adjacent to Sembuwatta Lake, a man-made lake created from natural spring water. The area is known for its rich biodiversity and serene environment, making it a popular spot for nature enthusiasts.​

gomare rock

20 Km away Gomara village historical site

Gomare Rock is a notable geological formation in the vicinity of the Knuckles Mountain Range. The area is known for its scenic beauty and is a popular spot for hikers and nature lovers.

Hunnasgiriya Mountain

30 Km away Knuckles Mountain Range historical site

Hunnasgiriya is a scenic region known for its lush green landscapes and cool climate. It serves as one of the entry points to the Knuckles massif, a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its biodiversity and trekking opportunities.
